My History & Profile

I am currently an osteoarchaeologist, re-training as a software developer at the Iron Yard in Central London. I have previously worked for various archaeological companies such as: MOLA and North Cornwall Heritage.

I first started university in 2006 at the University of Bristol, studying Chemistry. However, I did not find this to be entirely fulfilling and I left the course in 2008. I worked as an assistant science technician at University College School between the end of 2008 and 2009, when I decided to embark on a degree in Bioarchaeology at the University of York. During my time spent on the degree I specialized in human evolution, human osteology and paleodiet. After graduating in 2012, with a 2:1, I went on to start a masters degree in Skeletal and Dental Bioarchaeology at University College London. For the duration of the course I increased my knowledge of human osteology, and went on to focus on the study of the teeth.

After graduating from UCL I went on to work as an archaeologist all over the country but struggled to find work as an osteoarchaeologist. To supplement my income I also worked as a team assistant at an events agency called High Society.

When I am not studying code (HTML, CSS and JavaScript at the moment) or reading about the latest advancements in archaeological science, I enjoy many other activities. I like to stay active so I try and run at least a mile every day. I also play football and American (flag) football every week. I am a voracious reader, my favorite genres being fantasy and historical fiction. I am also an active video game player

and I especially enjoy sinking my teeth into a good RPG or JRPG.
